About Me

Hello! I am Ethan James German, a Ph.D. student at the University of Sheffield, specializing in black hole perturbation theory and fundamental fields on black hole spacetimes. I am passionate about exploring the mysteries of the cosmos through my research.

Research

In my current research, I delve into the intricate world of black hole perturbation theory, specifically focusing on the electromagnetic self-force. My primary objective is to comprehend how the electric interactions among charged particles influence their individual motion and inspiral, leading to distinctive behaviors compared to other particles. A noteworthy discovery in my work involves understanding how field interactions can dynamically alter the position of the turnaround radius—the point where cosmic expansion overcomes gravitational pull. This research not only contributes to our understanding of fundamental forces in extreme gravitational environments but also holds significant implications for gravitational wave waveform modeling. Looking ahead, my research trajectory includes a focus on calculating the conservative part of the electromagnetic self-force, paving the way for deeper insights into the dynamics of charged particles near black holes.
